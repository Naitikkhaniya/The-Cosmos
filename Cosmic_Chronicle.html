Cosmic Chronicles

Distant Galaxies

JWST is peering further into the universe than ever before, revealing galaxies from the very early universe. These observations are helping us understand how galaxies formed and evolved in the first billion years after the Big Bang.

Exoplanets

The search for exoplanets continues to yield exciting results, with a focus on planets orbiting M-dwarf stars. These systems may be more likely to host habitable planets.

Brown Dwarfs

Brown dwarfs, objects between planets and stars, are being studied in detail. Recent research has revealed complex weather patterns in their atmospheres.

Star Formation

Telescopes like ALMA and JWST are providing high-resolution views of star formation regions, revealing the processes by which stars form from clouds of gas and dust.

Nebulae

JWST's infrared vision is providing stunning new images of nebulae, revealing their intricate structures and the processes occurring within them.

Supernovae

Supernovae, powerful stellar explosions, are being studied to understand how heavy elements are created and distributed throughout the universe.

Near-Earth Asteroids

Tracking and characterizing near-Earth asteroids is vital for planetary defense. New discoveries are constantly improving our knowledge of their distribution and composition.

Gravitational Waves

The detection of gravitational waves has opened a new window into the universe, allowing us to study cataclysmic events like black hole mergers and neutron star collisions.

Cosmic Microwave Background

The cosmic microwave background radiation is the afterglow of the Big Bang, providing valuable information about the early universe.
